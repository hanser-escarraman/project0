Pop Rock

It is a musical genre that combines melodies and lyrics typical of pop music with elements typical of rock, such as a greater presence of electric guitars or a stronger rhythm. There are various definitions for the term ranging from a slower and smoother form of rock, to a subgenre within pop

Pop

is a genre of popular music that originated in the late 1950s as a derivation of traditional pop, combined with other musical genres that were in vogue at the time.1 2 The terms pop music and popular music are used often indistinctly, although the latter has a broader meaning by accommodating other genres other than pop that are considered popular.

Reggae

It is a musical genre that first developed in Jamaica in the mid-1970s. Although the term is sometimes used broadly to refer to different styles of Jamaican music, reggae is strictly understood to mean a specific musical genre that it originated as a development of previous ones such as ska and rocksteady

Merengue

It is a danceable genre of music originating in the Dominican Republic in the late 19th century. It is very popular throughout the American continent, where it is considered, along with salsa, as one of the great danceable musical genres that distinguish the Latin American genre. It is also very popular in part of Europe, such as Spain, among other latitudes. The merengue was inscribed on November 30, 2016 in the Unesco Representative List of the Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity.1
